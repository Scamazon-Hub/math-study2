Practical Problems – Maximum and Minimum

Optimization techniques in applied mathematics

Introduction to Maximum and Minimum Problems

The problems you are likely to encounter can be divided broadly into two types:

  • Type (a): Those involving a given function or expression
  • Type (b): Those requiring the expression or mathematical model to be deduced from given information and data

Key Insight

For type (b) problems, we need to devise the necessary function using a systematic approach:

  1. Write an expression for the required quantity
  2. Use the given conditions to rewrite it in terms of a single variable
  3. Find the maximum and/or minimum values by normal methods

Example 1: Maximum Rectangular Area

Find the area of the largest piece of rectangular ground that can be enclosed by 1000 m of fencing if part of an existing wall is used as one side.

Solution Approach

Let the area of the rectangle be \( A \, \text{m}^2 \).

1. Expression for area: \( A = xy \)

2. Fencing constraint: \( y = 1000 - 2x \)

3. Combined expression: \( A = x(1000 - 2x) \)

Expanding: \( A = 1000x - 2x^2 \)

Differentiating: \( \frac{dA}{dx} = 1000 - 4x \)

Second derivative: \( \frac{d^2A}{dx^2} = -4 \) (negative, so maximum)

Setting derivative to zero: \( 1000 - 4x = 0 \)

Solving: \( x = 250 \)

Maximum area: \( A = 250(1000 - 500) = 125,000 \, \text{m}^2 \)

The maximum area is 125,000 m² when the dimensions are 250 m × 500 m.

Example 2: Maximum Volume of Cylinder in Cone

Calculate the maximum volume of a cylinder which can be cut out of a cone of base radius 1 m and height 2 m.

Solution Approach

Volume of cylinder: \( V = \pi r^2 h \)

Using similar triangles: \( r = \frac{(2 - h)}{2} \) or \( h = 2 - 2r \)

Substituting: \( V = \pi r^2 (2 - 2r) = 2\pi r^2 - 2\pi r^3 \)

Differentiating: \( \frac{dV}{dr} = 4\pi r - 6\pi r^2 \)

Second derivative: \( \frac{d^2V}{dr^2} = 4\pi - 12\pi r \)

Setting derivative to zero: \( 4\pi r - 6\pi r^2 = 0 \)

Solving: \( r = \frac{2}{3} \) m (discarding r = 0)

Maximum volume: \( V = \pi \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^2 \left( 2 - \frac{4}{3} \right) \approx 0.931 \, \text{m}^3 \)

Example 3: Maximum Power in Resistor

The power \( P \) developed in a resistor \( R \) by a battery of e.m.f. \( E \) and internal resistance \( r \) is given by:

\[ P = \frac{E^2 R}{(R + r)^2} \]

Show that the power is a maximum when \( R = r \).

Solution Approach

Using quotient rule with \( u = E^2 R \) and \( v = (R + r)^2 \):

\( \frac{dP}{dR} = \frac{(R + r)^2(E^2) - (E^2 R)(2(R + r))}{(R + r)^4} \)

Simplifying: \( \frac{dP}{dR} = \frac{E^2(r - R)}{(R + r)^3} \)

Setting derivative to zero: \( E^2(r - R) = 0 \)

Since \( E^2 \neq 0 \), we get \( R = r \)

Verifying maximum:

When \( R < r \), \( \frac{dP}{dR} > 0 \)

When \( R > r \), \( \frac{dP}{dR} < 0 \)

Thus, maximum at \( R = r \)

Example 4: Pressure-Temperature Relationship

The pressure-temperature relationship for an organic vapour is given by:

\[ \frac{2a}{RT} - \frac{3abp}{R^2T^2} - b = 0 \]

Part (a): Express p in terms of T

Multiplying by \( R^2T^2 \): \( 2aRT - 3abp - bR^2T^2 = 0 \)

Rearranging: \( 3abp = 2aRT - bR^2T^2 \)

Solving for p: \( p = \frac{2RT}{3b} - \frac{R^2T^2}{3a} \)

Part (b): Show that p is maximum when \( T = \frac{a}{bR} \)

Differentiating: \( \frac{dp}{dT} = \frac{2R}{3b} - \frac{2R^2T}{3a} \)

Second derivative: \( \frac{d^2p}{dT^2} = -\frac{2R^2}{3a} \) (negative, so maximum)

Setting derivative to zero: \( \frac{2R}{3b} - \frac{2R^2T}{3a} = 0 \)

Solving: \( T = \frac{a}{bR} \)

Self Assessment Questions

Question 1: Minimum Surface Area of Cylinder

A closed cylindrical can has a height h and a radius r. If the capacity is 1 litre, show that:

\( h = \frac{1000}{\pi r^2} \) cm

Surface area: \( S = 2\pi r^2 + \frac{2000}{r} \) cm²

Find the value of r for which S is minimum.

Answer: Minimum when r = 5.42 cm

Question 2: Maximum Voltage

An electrical voltage E is given by:

\[ E = 10 \sin 20\pi t + 30 \cos 20\pi t \, \text{volts} \]

Determine the maximum voltage.

Answer: Maximum voltage = 31.62 volts

Question 3: Maximum Speed

The speed v (m/s) of a car is related to time t (seconds) by:

\[ v = 4 + 8t - 3t^2 \]

Determine the maximum speed in km/h.

Answer: Maximum speed = 33.6 km/h

Question 4: Maximum Bending Moment

The bending moment M of a beam of length l at a distance x from one end is given by:

\[ M = \frac{Wx}{2} (l - x) \]

where W is the load per unit length. Find the maximum bending moment.

Answer: Maximum bending moment = \( \frac{Wl^2}{8} \)

Summary of Techniques

Key Steps for Maximum/Minimum Problems

  1. Identify the quantity to be optimized
  2. Express this quantity as a function of one variable
  3. Differentiate the function
  4. Set the derivative equal to zero and solve
  5. Use the second derivative test to confirm maximum/minimum
  6. Calculate the optimal value
Problem Type Key Insight Common Applications
Geometric Optimization Use constraints to reduce variables Area, volume, packaging
Physical Systems Apply physical laws as constraints Mechanics, circuits, thermodynamics
Economic Optimization Balance cost and revenue functions Profit maximization, cost minimization
